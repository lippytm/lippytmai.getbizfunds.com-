# Autonomous AI Self-Improvement and Self-Healing Systems

## Purpose

This guide defines how autonomous AI systems can support safer self-improvement, self-healing, diagnostics, learning, and R&D across the wider ecosystem.

The goal is to make the system better at:
- detecting drift
- detecting weak or stale assets
- detecting broken handoffs
- improving documentation health
- improving CRM continuity
- improving asset reuse
- improving next-best actions
- restoring healthier system states after issues or confusion

---

## Core Principle

Self-improvement should be structured, documented, and reviewable.
Self-healing should restore clarity, continuity, and usable system states before adding new complexity.

---

## Self-Improvement Domains

### Domain 1 — Repo Surface Health
Improve repo role clarity, purpose visibility, status freshness, and standards coverage.

### Domain 2 — Asset Health
Improve prompts, schemas, templates, workflows, and guides so they remain reusable and current.

### Domain 3 — CRM Health
Improve lifecycle continuity, next actions, satisfaction visibility, and retention support.

### Domain 4 — Documentation Health
Improve transparency, ownership visibility, traceability, and scanability.

### Domain 5 — Fleet Health
Improve cross-repo visibility, reuse, auditability, and prioritization.

### Domain 6 — Learning Health
Improve lesson capture, promotion of durable knowledge, and prevention of stranded insights.

---

## Self-Healing Actions

Typical self-healing actions should include:
- restore missing status visibility
- restore missing next-best actions
- restore ownership visibility
- repair broken cross-repo references
- surface hidden reusable assets
- flag weak handoffs for human review
- re-index important prompts or docs
- promote durable lessons into stronger long-term repos

---

## Best Practices
- heal visibility before attempting deeper optimization
- prefer small structured corrections over vague large changes
- keep all upgrades documented in reviewable formats
- preserve transparency around why a change is recommended
- use human review for high-impact shared-standard changes when needed

---

## Strategic Outcome

This guide supports a healthier autonomous ecosystem that can diagnose itself better, improve itself more safely, and recover from drift or weak system states with stronger documentation and governance.
