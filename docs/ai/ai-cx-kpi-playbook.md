# AI CX KPI Playbook

## Purpose

This playbook explains how to use customer experience and client experience KPIs to improve clarity, continuity, satisfaction, trust, support quality, retention, and reusable system learning across the wider ecosystem.

## Core Principle

KPIs should not exist only for observation.
They should support better diagnosis, better next actions, and better customer and client outcomes.

## Main KPI Families

### Family 1 — First Touch and Clarity
Use KPIs such as:
- first_touch_clarity_score
- next_action_visibility_score
- intake_completeness_rate

Why it matters:
- improves early confidence
- reduces confusion
- improves conversion readiness

### Family 2 — Continuity and Handoffs
Use KPIs such as:
- handoff_context_quality
- lifecycle_stage_freshness
- crm_next_action_quality

Why it matters:
- preserves trust
- reduces repeated explanation
- improves continuity across humans and AI

### Family 3 — Support and Recovery
Use KPIs such as:
- support_resolution_confidence
- recovery_followthrough_rate
- trust_risk_visibility

Why it matters:
- improves issue recovery
- reduces silent damage to relationships
- improves long-term loyalty

### Family 4 — Satisfaction and Retention
Use KPIs such as:
- satisfaction_signal_strength
- churn_risk_visibility
- renewal_readiness_signal
- referral_readiness_signal

Why it matters:
- detects risk earlier
- supports better timing for loyalty and advocacy
- strengthens retention and expansion

### Family 5 — Documentation and Transparency
Use KPIs such as:
- documentation_transparency_score
- asset_visibility_score
- canonical_ownership_visibility

Why it matters:
- improves scanability
- improves AI and human continuation quality
- reduces hidden system drift

## KPI Review Rhythm

### Weekly
Focus on:
- next action visibility
- stale lifecycle states
- support recovery risks
- urgent churn risk signals

### Monthly
Focus on:
- satisfaction signal strength
- documentation transparency
- handoff quality
- referral and renewal readiness

### Quarterly
Focus on:
- KPI usefulness
- definitions that need refinement
- which KPIs produce real action vs noise
- cross-repo KPI consistency

## Best Practices
- prefer fewer useful KPIs over many weak KPIs
- tie every KPI family to concrete actions
- improve visibility before inventing more metrics
- use KPI review to strengthen customer and client experience, not only reporting

## Strategic Outcome

This playbook helps the ecosystem use CX and CRM KPIs as a practical improvement system rather than a passive reporting layer.
