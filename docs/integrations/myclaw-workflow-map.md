# MyClaw Workflow Map

## Purpose

Map how website visitors from `lippytmai.getbizfunds.com` can be routed into MyClaw workflows for intake, follow-up, categorization, and next-step handling.

## Core Workflow Categories

### 1. Business Funding Help
**Trigger:** visitor selects funding-related help on the website.

**Suggested workflow:**
- capture core contact details
- record business stage
- record short summary of current need
- assign funding-pathway tag
- send acknowledgment follow-up
- queue deeper readiness questions or manual review

### 2. AI Business Setup
**Trigger:** visitor selects AI setup or AI business system support.

**Suggested workflow:**
- capture business type
- capture current tool use
- capture desired AI outcome
- assign AI-setup tag
- send short follow-up explaining next step
- route toward consultation, intake, or implementation planning

### 3. Automation Services
**Trigger:** visitor requests workflow or automation help.

**Suggested workflow:**
- capture process pain point
- identify current manual steps
- assign automation-interest tag
- send follow-up about workflow discovery
- route to process review or strategy step

### 4. Strategy and Planning
**Trigger:** visitor requests business direction or growth planning help.

**Suggested workflow:**
- capture business stage
- capture primary goal
- assign strategy tag
- offer session request or guided next step

### 5. Affiliate / Partnership Interest
**Trigger:** visitor selects ecosystem, partnership, or collaboration interest.

**Suggested workflow:**
- capture interest type
- capture relevant links or business details
- assign affiliate-or-partnership tag
- route to nurture list or manual review

### 6. General Inquiry
**Trigger:** visitor submits non-categorized request.

**Suggested workflow:**
- capture standard contact details
- assign general inquiry tag
- route to manual review or general support queue

## Shared Workflow Elements

Across all categories, consider using:
- timestamp of submission
- source page or landing page
- traffic source when available
- category tag
- stage tag
- follow-up status
- priority status

## Follow-Up Principles

- first response should be fast and simple
- deeper questions should come after first contact
- category-specific follow-up is better than one universal follow-up
- keep human review available for unclear or higher-value leads

## Best Practices

- build modular workflows by category
- avoid overcomplicating the first version
- keep form-to-workflow handoff clean
- tag leads in a way that supports reporting later
- document each workflow in the repo so the site and operations stay aligned
